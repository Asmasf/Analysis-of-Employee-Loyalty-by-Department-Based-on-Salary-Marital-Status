# 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee loyalty based on salary level, department, and marital status using Human Resources (HR) data.

The objective is to identify factors that influence employee retention and provide insights that can help organizations improve workforce stability and reduce turnover.

---

# ❗ Problem Statement

Employee turnover can increase recruitment costs and reduce organizational productivity.

Understanding the factors that influence employee loyalty is essential for developing effective retention strategies and maintaining a stable workforce.

---

# ❓ Business Questions

* Does salary influence employee loyalty?
* Does marital status affect employee loyalty?
* Which department has the highest employee retention?
* Which employee groups are most at risk of turnover?

---

# 📊 Dataset

* Source: Kaggle
* Records: 3,000 employees
* Features: 26 columns

Key variables:

* Salary Level
* Department
* Employee Type
* Marital Status
* Employment Status
* Start Date & Exit Date

# 📈 Key Insights

### 1. Salary Is Not the Main Driver of Loyalty

Employee loyalty is relatively balanced across salary categories, indicating that factors beyond compensation influence retention.

### 2. Married Employees Are More Loyal

Employees with family responsibilities tend to prioritize job stability and show higher loyalty levels.

### 3. Production Has the Strongest Retention

The Production department has the largest number of loyal employees.

### 4. Software Engineering Faces Retention Challenges

Software Engineering shows a higher proportion of non-loyal employees, indicating potential retention issues.

---

# 💡 Recommendations

* Strengthen career development programs.
* Improve employee engagement initiatives.
* Conduct retention analysis within Software Engineering.
* Provide mentoring and career support programs for employees with higher turnover risk.

---

# 📌 Conclusion

Employee loyalty is influenced more by career stability, personal circumstances, and departmental culture than by salary alone.

Organizations should focus on employee development, engagement, and retention strategies to build a more loyal workforce.
